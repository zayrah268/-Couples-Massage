Dual Massage is often used as a broader term. It simply means two massages happening at the same time, usually in the same room. What Happens in a Couples Massage Session?

A Couples Massage Session refers to the actual massage appointment, including the setup, consultation, and the massage treatment itself. Most sessions begin with a short consultation, where therapists ask about your preferred pressure level, pain points, and any health concerns.

Once the session begins, both individuals lie on separate massage tables. Each person gets their own therapist, and the massage starts at the same time. Depending on the spa, couples can choose the same massage type or different ones. Some people prefer Swedish massage for relaxation, while the other may choose deep tissue massage for muscle pain.

The session is typically calm and quiet, allowing both partners to fully relax. After the massage, many spas offer a short relaxation period, herbal tea, or aftercare guidance to complete the wellness experience.

Popular Massage Options in Couples or Dual Sessions

Couples massage sessions can include different massage styles depending on the spa. Swedish massage is one of the most common choices because it is gentle, relaxing, and perfect for people trying massage for the first time. Deep tissue massage is more intense and is often chosen by those experiencing stiffness or chronic muscle pain.

Hot stone massage is another popular option because the warmth helps muscles loosen quickly and provides deep relaxation. Aromatherapy massage uses essential oils to create emotional calmness and is often preferred by couples looking for a more soothing, romantic experience. Many spas also offer customized packages where couples can mix different massage styles in the same session.

Who Should Avoid Couples Massage?

Couples massage is generally safe, but people with certain conditions should consult a doctor before booking. For example, individuals with high fever, infections, recent surgery, severe osteoporosis, or blood clot issues should avoid massage therapy. Pregnant individuals should choose prenatal massage and ensure the spa has therapists trained specifically for pregnancy massage.
